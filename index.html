How it works

  1. The water is filled with nutrients required for plants to grow. They usually come from fecal matter or chemical fertilisers.
  2. The nutrients are then consumed from the plants that are used to make food.
  3. The filtered water returns in the tank to repeat the process again.

How it works

  1. The fish in the tank let out ammonia, a by-product for many animals. It is carried in the water and goes towards the plants

  2. The water is then filtered from most large solids to prevent the system from getting clogged. The plant's nutrition does not come from the solids, but are contained within the water.

  3. In the growbed, a bacteria called nitrosomosis digests that product to turn it into nitrites. Then, it gets oxidised into nitrates, a valuable macronutrient for many plants.

  4. The water filtered by the plants is then returned to the fish, repeating the process indefinitely
